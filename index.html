About Nebula Neuro

We build infrastructure that lets scientists anchor their experiments, analyses, and results directly to blockchain-based ledgers — without turning labs into full-time Web3 developers.

Our goal is simple: make it trivial for a research group to prove what was done, when, with which parameters and which data, while keeping sensitive information off-chain and under the lab’s control.

Built for scientists, not speculators

We start from the realities of experimental science: messy data, evolving protocols, time pressure, and limited engineering support.

  • Versioned experiment definitions and pipelines
  • Cryptographic hashes of raw and processed data
  • Permissioned sharing for collaborators and reviewers

Protocol: chains that understand experiments

Under the hood, we combine content-addressable storage, reproducible computation, and smart contracts to track how scientific results are produced and reused.

On-chain provenance graph

Every registered experiment updates a provenance graph: who ran what, when, with which inputs. The chain stores hashes and metadata, while bulk data stays in lab-controlled storage.

Reproducible pipelines

Dockerized or containerized analysis steps get pinned with exact versions. Anyone can verify that a reported figure could be regenerated from the same inputs and code.

Programmable access

Smart contracts govern who can discover, request, or compute on datasets. Labs retain control while enabling automated meta-analysis, benchmarking, and replication studies.

Applications

From single-lab projects to multi-site consortia, we help make scientific outputs machine-checkable and economically reusable.

Registered analyses

Lock in analysis plans, parameter ranges, and data inclusion criteria before you touch the data, and anchor them to a chain to prevent quiet garden-of-forking-paths exploration.

Reproducible data releases

Ship datasets with a formal, on-chain provenance trail. Let others recompute results or extend your pipelines without losing track of where each artifact came from.

Incentivized replication

Use smart contracts and tokens to reward independent replications, meta-analyses, and robustness checks instead of only rewarding the first flashy result.
